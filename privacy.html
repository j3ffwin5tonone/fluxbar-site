Privacy Policy for FluxBar

Last updated: 07.12.2025

1. General Information

FluxBar is a macOS application that displays local system information such as CPU usage, memory consumption, and network activity directly in the macOS menu bar. FluxBar is designed with a strong focus on privacy and minimalism.

2. No Data Collection

FluxBar does not collect, store, process, transmit, or share any personal data. All system metrics displayed by FluxBar remain on your device. No analytics, telemetry, or data logging is performed.

FluxBar does not contact external servers, cloud services, or third-party APIs.

3. No Internet Communication

FluxBar functions entirely offline. The application does not require an internet connection to operate and will not send data to external destinations under any circumstances.

4. Information Displayed

FluxBar reads system information provided by macOS, including (but not limited to):

  • CPU load
  • Memory usage
  • Network activity
  • System uptime

This information is displayed locally and is never transmitted outside of your device.

5. No Tracking, No Cookies

FluxBar does not use cookies, session storage, SDKs, fingerprinting technologies, advertising networks, or tracking services of any kind.

6. Third-Party Access

FluxBar does not integrate any third-party libraries or external analytics tools that process user data.

7. Legal Basis (GDPR / DSGVO)

Since FluxBar does not process or store personal data, the data processing definitions of the GDPR (Art. 4 No. 1 and No. 2) do not apply.

No personal data is collected, and therefore no legal basis under Art. 6 GDPR is required.
